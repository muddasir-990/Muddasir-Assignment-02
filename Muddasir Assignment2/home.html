History of EV Bikes

  • 1890s: The first patents for electric bicycles appeared in the U.S., including designs with batteries and motors.

  • 1990s: E-bikes began gaining traction in Asia, especially in China, due to urban congestion and pollution concerns.

  • 2000s: Technological advances in lithium-ion batteries made e-bikes lighter, more efficient, and practical.

  • 2010s: E-bike popularity surged globally, especially in Europe, for commuting and leisure; companies like Bosch, Shimano, and Yamaha developed advanced drive systems.

  • 2020spresent: E-bikes became mainstream, with a boom during the COVID-19 pandemic; governments began promoting them for sustainable transport, and innovations in design, battery life, and connectivity continue to evolve.
